Daniel K. Inouye

Story about a heart of valor

Sergeant Inouye looking forward in the Vosges Mountains with gun in hand

Battle at Monte Cassino

April 21, 1945

  • Lieutenant Inouye led a flanking attack on a heavily-defended ridge near San Terenzo in Tuscany, Italy.
  • Platoon surprise ambushed by 3 machine guns at close range
  • Inouye stood up to look for cover but was shot in stomach
  • Ignoring his wound, attacked and destroyed 1st machine gun nest with grenades and gun
  • Rallied men to 2nd machine gun position, successfully destroyed it, collapsed from blood loss.
  • Crawled toward final bunker, raised himself up and cocked a grenade in hand. Was shot by German soldier at the right elbow and lost his arm.
  • Ripped out grenade from severed arm and threw it into bunker before chance of being shot again by German soldier.
  • After silencing the last German resistance, fell unconscious into a ridge.

For his valor in this battle and throughout the war, he was awarded:

  • Medal of Honor
  • Bronze Star Medal
  • Purple Heart
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal
  • World War II Victory Medal
